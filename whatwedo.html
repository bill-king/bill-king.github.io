What We Do

Volunteers are the heart of the Mariposa Trails Community, shaping its vision, enlivening its mission, and achieving its goals. We are inclusive in our support for trail access on behalf of hikers, runners, cyclists, equestrians and others who use trails for outdoor recreation. Mariposa Trails is an unincorporated non-profit (working toward 501(3)c status) working to accomplish objectives in five goal areas: (1) Tending the Trails; (2) Fitness & Community Outreach; (3) Support the Local Economy; (4) Youth Pathways; and, (5) Advocacy and Planning.

Mariposa Trails Working Groups


Tending the Trails

While Mariposa County is home to Yosemite National Park, it also contains other public lands that are closer to its citizens and less crowded. Mariposa Trails has stepped up to tend front-country trails for the enjoyment of all types of nature-based recreation, including hikers, cyclists, runners, equestrian and off-highway vehicles. Most outings are in the fall and winter, when the soil is soft, the air is cool, and bugs are nowhere to be seen!

Fitness and Community Outreach

The opportunity to enjoy the natural world by residents and tourists alike is a big reason why Mariposa Trails creates and maintains a trail network. Coupled with this work, Mariposa Trails actively collaborates and engages the community to recreate on these trails through sponsored hikes and tours.



Support the Local Economy

The economy of Mariposa is rooted in the abundant richness of its land that attracts and nourishes tourists and residents alike. These lands provide opportunities for trail-based activities, such as endurance runs, bicycle races and multisport events, and associated enterprises. Proceeds from these events are used by Mariposa Trails to maintain and improve the trail network, as well as fund our other programs and initiatives.



Youth Pathways

Mariposa Trails seeks to involve local youth and area college students in its endeavors to improve their physical and mental health, and to expand their knowledge and skills. Mariposa County High School students crafted the post-fire South Fork Trail Signs, and students from Central Valley Universities participate in annual multi-day trail stewardship outings.

Advocacy & Planning

The condition of trails in the field is largely defined by the attention given to them in official government planning-related documents. Hence, Mariposa Trails seeks to form strong and positive partnerships with government agencies; to be active participants in the development and implementation of planning documents; and, to encourage and support community and local partner involvement in all planning efforts related to the construction of local trails in a sustainable manner.
