What We Do


Tend the Trails

While Mariposa County is home to Yosemite National Park, it also contains other public lands that are closer to its citizens and less crowded. Mariposa Trails has stepped up to tend front-country trails for the enjoyment of all types of nature-based recreation, including hikers, cyclists, runners and equestrians. Most outings are in the fall and winter, when the soil is soft, the air is cool, and bugs are nowhere to be seen!



Create Outdoor Recreational Opportunities

The livelihood of Mariposa County is rooted in the abundant richness of its land, which attracts and nourishes tourists and residents alike. Recognizing this, MARIPOSA TRAILS aims to provide access to outdoor recreational trails for the community. Our strategy is two-fold: tend the trails AND promote their use. To this end, we organize hiking, backpacking, biking and running trips, as well as race events. Proceeds from these activities are invested in trail network improvements and recreation and education programs.



Engage Youth

Mariposa Trails seeks to involve local youth and area college students in its endeavors to improve their physical and mental health, and to expand their knowledge and skills. For example, Mariposa County High School students crafted the post-fire South Fork Trail Signs, and students from Central Valley Universities regularly participate in multi-day trail stewardship outings.



Advocate for Trails

The condition of trails is largely defined by the attention given to them by government agencies and their planning documents. Hence, Mariposa Trails seeks to form strong and positive partnerships with government agencies; to be active participants in the development and implementation of planning documents; and, to encourage and support community and local partner involvement in all planning efforts related to local trails.
