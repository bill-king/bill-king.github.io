What We Do

Volunteers are the heart of the Mariposa Trails Community, enlivening its mission and realizing its goals. We are inclusive in our support for volunteering, collaborating and advocating for trail access on behalf of hikers, runners, bikers, equestrians and all others who use trails for outdoor recreation. To achieve our, Mariposa Trails:

  • Build and maintain safe and sustainable trails for all types of nature-based recreation
  • Support local partners with trail planning, construction and maintenance services
  • Engage the local community to recreate on trails in Mariposa County
  • Host race events on local trails to promote the local economy
  • Educate youth using trail work, events and advocacy as platforms for this initiative
  • Advocate for new trail access in a sustainable manner
  • Collaborate with community organizations to enhance trail-based recreation for everyone
  • Raise funds to implement to goals above.

Tending the Trails

While Mariposa is home to Yosemite National Park, it also contains national forest recreational lands that are closer and less crowded. As the trails in these lands are in desperate need of stewardship, Mariposa Trails has stepped up to improve and care for the trails for enjoyment of all user types, including hikers, cyclists, runners, equestrian and off-highway vehicles. Trail work occurs from a vehicle basecamp, by backpack or through day outings

Healthy People & Healthy Trails Initiative

Enjoyment of the forest, rivers, woodlands by residents and tourists alike is a big reason why Mariposa Trails creates and maintain a trail network. As an athlete “follows through,” so does Mariposa Trails by actively promoting recreational use of the trails through sponsored hikes and tours.



Growing the Local Economy

The economy of Mariposa is strongly rooted in the abundant richness of its land, attracting and nourishing both tourists and residents alike. Lands outside Yosemite National Park that are closer, less crowded, and inviting during the fall, winter and spring, provide opportunities for homegrown trail-based activities such as endurance runs, bicycle races and multisport events, as well as related industries. Mariposa Trails uses proceeds from these events to maintain, improve and grow the trail network.



Youth Pathways

Mariposa Trails seeks to include local youth in all of its endeavors in a structured manner to expand their knowledge, skills and experience. In 2019, Mariposa County High School students crafted new South Fork Trail Signs. In 2018 and 2019, students from Central Valley Universities participated in multi-day trail stewardship outings.

Advocacy And Planning

The condition of trails in the field is largely defined by the status afforded them in the plans that sit in government offices. Hence, Mariposa Trails seeks to form strong positive partnerships with government agencies, to be active participants in the development and implementation of planning documents, and to encourage community involvement in all planning efforts related to local trails
