Mariposa Trails Working Groups

Mariposa Trails is a non-profit organization whose volunteers are at its heart, shaping its vision, enlivening its mission, and achieving its goal to maintain and promote recreational trail access for hikers, runners, cyclists, equestrians and others by tending the trails, promoting fitness & community, expanding the local economy, teaching youth, and advocating for trails


Tend the Trails

While Mariposa County is home to Yosemite National Park, it also contains other public lands that are closer to its citizens and less crowded. Mariposa Trails has stepped up to tend front-country trails for the enjoyment of all types of nature-based recreation, including hikers, cyclists, runners, equestrian and off-highway vehicles. Most outings are in the fall and winter, when the soil is soft, the air is cool, and bugs are nowhere to be seen!

Promote Fitness and Community

The opportunity to enjoy the natural world by residents and tourists alike is a big reason why Mariposa Trails creates and maintains a trail network. Coupled with this work, Mariposa Trails actively collaborates and engages the community to recreate on these trails through sponsored hikes and tours.

Expand the Local Economy

The economy of Mariposa is rooted in the abundant richness of its land, whivh attracts and nourishes tourists and residents alike. These lands provide opportunities for trail-based activities, such as endurance runs, bicycle races, multisport events, and associated enterprises. Proceeds from these events are used by Mariposa Trails to maintain and improve the trail network, as well as fund its programs and initiatives.

Teach Youth

Mariposa Trails seeks to involve local youth and area college students in its endeavors to improve their physical and mental health, and to expand their knowledge and skills. For example, Mariposa County High School students crafted the post-fire South Fork Trail Signs, and students from Central Valley Universities regularly participate in multi-day trail stewardship outings.

Advocate for Trails

The condition of trails is largely defined by the attention given to them by government agencies and their planning documents. Hence, Mariposa Trails seeks to form strong and positive partnerships with government agencies; to be active participants in the development and implementation of planning documents; and, to encourage and support community and local partner involvement in all planning efforts related to local trails.
