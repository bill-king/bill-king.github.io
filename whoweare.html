Our Vision

Foundational to the work of MARIPOSA TRAILS is the ethic of "taking" and "giving" from the land. MARIPOSA TRAILS recognizes the value of gifts from nature, knowing that our health, prosperity and enjoyment are rooted in our access to the earth, and our care of it. Instilled in our work is the notion of "paying it forward,‚Äù We believe trails are pathways to adventure, exploration and discovery of nature and our place in it.



Mission Statement

The mission of MARIPOSA TRAILS is to be stewards of a trail network, emphasizing them as pathways of discovery of nature and our place in it, and promoting their use for personal health and community prosperity.



Community Volunteers, Advisory Council and Fiduciary

Volunteers are the heart of the Mariposa Trails organization, shaping its vision, enlivening its mission, and achieving its goal to maintain and promote recreational trail access for hikers, runners, cyclists and equestrians. A council of trail advocates advises and guides Mariposa Trails founder Bill King. As the organization grows and obtains incorporated non-profit status, a board of directors will be formed. The Upper Merced Watershed Council acts as the group fiduciary.
